Customer insights in information systems

Customer insights refer to customer analytics and market research data, to better understand the needs, wants and behaviours of customers. It allows the business to notice patterns, for example, producing jackets in the transition of summer to autumn.


Both quantitative and qualitative data is collected when reviewing customer insights, a business utilizes the following in order to gain customer insights:

Online Reviews ~ Online reviews give a direct voice to the consumer, this data is valuable as it comes straight from the customer and outlines the effectiveness of the product, and where it can be improved.


Social media platforms ~ Social media acts as a great way to obtain customer insight, it includes all from customer praise to their complaints and uses of the products. The business can gain a better understanding of the customers views on the products and what they compare the product to.


Website data ~ Website data can prove to be useful in the aspect of cookies. Internet cookies are data that is communicated with a website and a user’s browser, and it has the purpose to track the users interactions and preferences on the website. Cookies can be utilized by the website owner to understand how people found their website, how many times a user has visited their website, and how many and what pages the user looked at. These are just a few examples of how websites can be used for analytics in customer insights.

How does information systems relate to customer insights?

Data formed from customer insights ~ Information systems have a vital role in the success of customer insights, they collect, analyse, manage, and store data about customer interactions and preferences. Through the use of this information, it can allow a business to satisfy the demand efficiently and effectively.


Customer Relationships Management Software: This software is designed to manage the interactions with the customer, track communication and provide a central place to view and manipulate this information to provide personalized services and products.


Communication: Information systems can provide communication with the customers ~ through websites, social media, email, chatbots. Businesses can interact with their customers simply while increasing customer satisfaction and loyalty.


Predictions: Information systems can employ data analysis techniques which could predict customer behaviour, this is due to it gathering information and identifying trends. This can allow for a business to tailor the needs of a customer to a specific product or service.
