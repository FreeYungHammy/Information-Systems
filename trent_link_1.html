Here is how we can achieve this in a IT business:

  1. IT Integration: Supply chains involve a network of suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, and retailers. IT systems like ERP software serve as the backbone, connecting these entities. These systems manage orders, inventory, and distribution efficiently.

  2. Real-time Tracking: Modern SCM heavily relies on IT for real-time tracking. RFID and IoT sensors enable organizations to monitor the movement of goods, providing visibility into their location and condition.

  3. Demand Forecasting: IT uses historical data and predictive analytics to forecast demand accurately. Machine learning models can adjust these forecasts in real-time, reducing overstock or stockouts.

  4. Supplier Collaboration: Cloud-based collaboration platforms facilitate communication with suppliers. IT enables collaborative demand planning, sharing inventory data, and coordinating production schedules.

  5. Blockchain: Blockchain technology enhances transparency and security. Each transaction in the supply chain is recorded in an immutable ledger, reducing the risk of fraud and errors.
